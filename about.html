About Us

A Center For Social Ecology and Free-Open Technology

The Unatural Speed of Life

In the last decades we have witnessed the intensification of production due the current consumption patterns that demand more resources. The pollution resulted from all of the current industrial-chain activities is jeopardizing the ecosystemic dynamic. The world today faces a growing environmental crisis and climate change is already a reality, with many extreme events being experienced around the globe: rainfall alteration, drought, or loss of biodiversity.

The Root of the Crisis

These problems are not just a matter of the so-called ‘nature’, but are driven by modern societal dynamic and affect nature as well as the society itself. The human's existence and survival had became dependent of technological devices ever more complex and we find ourselves entrapped inside a technological megastructure aiming both human and ecological exploitation.

The ecological crisis is deeply-rooted in social problems and is maintained by the current social and environmental alienation, the lack of sovereignty and autonomy on resources management, food production, environmental conservation, health and technology.

WHAT WE STAND FOR

Collective Action for Collective Problems

It is urgent to pursue an epistemological change that understands the environment as a whole ecosystem, namely an ecosocial system with decentralized inter-independent parts. We consider collective learning, a dialogue of knowledge for a wider understanding, as crucial for building the bases for social autonomy.

Thriving into free tools, that promote communities’ sovereignty, is the key to move towards a real change. Cal Teixidor (the weaver’s house in Catalan) aims to trigger a network of inter-independent projects that can be performed all around in any town, neighborhood or community considering their realities. Our purpose is to develop projects gathering together technology, grassroots participation and ecology in which freedom and autonomy can flourish. We are founded on three pillars: the environment, free-libre and open technologies, and agroecology. Cal Teixidor is a culmination of years of environmental studies, experience in agroecology, and development of free technology with a social approach. The integration of these aspects allows to connect the nodes and expand the edges to build a real transformation.
